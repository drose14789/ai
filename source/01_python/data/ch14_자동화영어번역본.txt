Tigers are the largest and most powerful carnivores of the feline family, and they have lived throughout Asia. With a muscular body and beautiful striped fur, tigers have agile and strong hunting abilities. They hunt large-scale herbivores, mainly deer and wild boars, and live alone. Tigers are efficient hunters, who use their excellent vision, hearing, and smell to track prey and terminate a hunt with a single powerful attack. Since ancient times, it has been regarded as a symbol of bravery, power, and protection in Eastern cultures, including Korea, and has often appeared as a sacred being in folk paintings and folktales.However, due to reckless poaching and habitat destruction, wild tigers are now critically endangered. Conservation programs and breeding studies are being conducted in many countries to protect them, and the tiger remains a symbolic animal that reminds humans of the dignity of nature and the value of respect for life.
